Speakers

  • Diana Hartig

    Raising Vegan Kids

    I am sharing what being vegan is like for my family and how we navigate the vegan lifestyle with our children. Some moments make vegan parenting challenging, such as going to birthday parties, seeing other kids eating meat, feeling excluded and being criticized by others. But there are also many good moments that I want to talk about with you.

    Hi, my name is Diana. I am a jewelry artist and a digital marketer. I have recently launched my compassionate clothing brand, 'Karmavore.' I integrate my vegan lifestyle into my professional life as much as possible. But besides being a busy work bee, I am a mom of two vegan boys. Being vegan myself hasn't been difficult, but once you become a parent, it is not just about you anymore. Suddenly, there is a small, innocent child who is entirely dependent on you to make choices in their best interest, their health and well-being. Raising vegan kids is challenging indeed, but it is also rewarding. I am happy to share some real-life experiences as a vegan family.

The tasteful melodies from "Copernicus" found their way in the harmonic foundations of "Downtown". The universe was pleased, a bond was made and Daiglo was named. As the months went by, Daiglo, determined to discover the truths of music, explored soulful creations and discoveries from the giants of the past. From what the giants left, Daiglo looked up and said "Thank you" and took their teachings with humility and gratitude. Now, it is time for Daiglo to grow, and to find their own way through this journey of life, harmony, melody and rhythm.

    Ma’iingan Miikana is the stage name of Wil Reed, the name is Ojibwe in origin, it means the Wolfs Trail and is symbolism for the suns path through the sky. Originally from Manitoba Wil is a member of the Peguis First Nation reserve Ma’iingan Miikana writes spoken word poetry and is a hip hop lyricist. The subject matter of his lyrics is of a wide variety including: the occult (occult means hidden), natural law, hermetic principles, ancestral trauma, the psychological shadow, exposing dark occultism, the solar and galactic spirals of creation and Conscious messages dealing with the great work of bio spiritual alchemical transformation. Ma’iingan does not shy away from speaking on the cause and effects of one’s inner darkness (ie subconscious and unconscious programs/distortions) and ignorance upon their own spiritual progression, nor is he one sided, both the light and the dark are explored through his lyrics.
