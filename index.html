Speakers

  • Vesanto Melina

    Plant Powered Protein: For People and the Planet

    Choosing plant protein foods over animal proteins provides powerful health advantages and can have a significant effect on climate change (greenhouse gas emissions, water use and pollution; land use and acidification). In this presentation, Vesanto Melina will answer many of your most pressing questions about protein from plants. Do vegans and vegetarians get enough protein? If we adopt a plant-based diet do we need to carefully combine beans and grains? Can top athletes compete successfully when their diets include no animal products? Are plant-exclusive diets suitable for every stage of the life cycle? Can seniors avoid sarcopenia (muscle wasting) while eating only plants? What are s impacts of going plant-based?

    Vesanto Melina, MS, Registered Dietitian is co-author of books that have won awards internationally and received star rating by the American Library Association as “the go-to books” on plant-based nutrition. Most recent are Plant-Powered Protein, The Kick Diabetes Cookbook, Becoming Vegan (Comprehensive and Express Edition), and Becoming Raw (all with dietitian Brenda Davis). Their books are now in 10 languages. Vesanto has taught nutrition at the University of British Columbia and at Seattle’s Bastyr University. She is the lead author of the American Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics latest Position Paper on Vegetarian Diets. She is an internationally known speaker (in 11 countries).
